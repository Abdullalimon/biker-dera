Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Helmet is older than 5 years. ... The interiors have started to come off. ... The exterior is showing signs of wear & tear. ... You can't get the stink off. ... The helmet wobbles.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

That depends upon it's condition before storage, what was done to prepare it for storage, how dry the storage was and how long it was in storage. Probably not totally safe. It likely needs air and for various fittings, nuts and bolts to be checked.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Stop before you get tired. This is different for different riders. I go 200–250 between stops. Don't stop when you're exhausted, but stop the moment things get sore and your hands get stiff. Don't risk an accident. Also, you'll be less tired at the end of the trip.
